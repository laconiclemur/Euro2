Traditional Nuremberg Pfefferkuchen

 

Did you know that the word by word translation means pepper cakes?  Liesel loves the Christmas fragrance in the house when baking these.

 

·         4 eggs

·         1 3/4 cups sugar

·         1 Tbsp. lemon peel

·         1/3 cup candied lemon peel

·         1/4 cup candied orange peel

·         5 tsps. gingerbread spice mix

·         1 scant cup chopped hazelnuts

·         1 scant cup chopped almonds

·         a pinch of Hirschhornsalz (or a pinch of soda and a pinch of baking powder)

·         1 Tbsp. rum

·         1 1/3 cup all-purpose flour

·         Small wafers for the bottom, optional

 

Beat eggs and sugar until fluffy.  Add lemon peel, candied lemon and orange peel, spice mix and the nuts.  Dissolve the Hirschhornsalz in the rum and add to the mixture.  Add the flour, working everything into a smooth dough.  Cover the bowl and keep refrigerated over night.

 

The next day, preheat the oven to 355° F.  Set small mounds of the dough on the wafers or parchment paper.  Bake for about 20 minutes.
