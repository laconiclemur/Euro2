Gingerbread Baking

 

What would Germany look like without gingerbread?  From the storied gingerbread house of Hansel und Gretel to gingerbread hearts and the world-famous Nuremberger Lebkuchen - there would be quite an empty space in the baking world.

 

At every Volksfest in the summer, at many Bierfests, at the Oktoberfest, and of course at all Christkindl markets, men buy gingerbread hearts for their girlfriends.  The hearts are decorated with sugary flowers and sentiments like "Be mine" and "I love you."

 

Before Christmas, gingerbread manufacturers in Nuremberg send beautiful collectible tins all over the world filled with a variety of delicious treats. Just to name a few, there are Pfeffernüsse, Dominosteine and Elisen Lebkuchen.  Some are covered in chocolate, others are just glazed.

 

When baking gingerbread at home, many recipes require "Hirschhornsalz" or "Hartshorn" as a leavening agent and "Backoblaten" as a base to spread the dough on top before baking.

 

Hirschhornsalz is ammonium bicarbonate, which can be found possibly at a compounding pharmacy.  A small bag of Hirschhornsalz can be purchased in German stores and on-line.  A substitute for Hirschhornsalz is equal parts baking powder and baking soda.  It might not turn out quite as airy or as crisp, but it will do nicely.

 

Backoblaten look like large communion wafers and come in different sizes.  My friend Liesel says baking gingerbread without Backoblaten works fine;  just spread the dough on parchment paper.

 

Gingerbread spice contains cinnamon, orange peel, lemon peel, star anise, fennel, cardamom, coriander, cloves, nutmeg and mace.  The most convenient way is to purchase a bag of mixed "Lebkuchen - Gewürz" or gingerbread spice.  One small bag of 15 grams (1/2 oz.,) contains 3 generous tablespoons of ground spices and flavors 2 cups flour, according to the bag.

 

Storing finished gingerbread is best in a tin at high humidity and a very cool temperature.  Should it get hard, add one or two apples to the tin and in a few days, the gingerbread will be soft again.
