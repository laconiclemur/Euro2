Springerle

 

Joni received this cookie from a friend in the Stuttgart area.  The Springerle recipe is very old and has been baked for centuries.  In olden times, rich people sacrificed animals to hope for a mild winter.  For poor people this was not an option, so instead they baked the Springerle with pictures of animals on it.  Traditionally, the cookies are rolled out with a wooden roller or pressed into tiles or molds, in which pictures are carved.  The finished cookies can be painted with acrylic paints when they are used only as decorations.

 

Don't put away the standing mixer yet, as the dough is very heavy.

 

·         4 large eggs

·         4 1/2 cups powdered sugar

·         dash salt

·         1 tsp. vanilla

·         4 cups flour, sifted

·         1 tsp. baking powder

·         1 Tbsp. soft butter

·         1 1/2 Tbsps. honey

·         anise seeds to sprinkle on the cookie sheets

 

In a stand mixer, beat the eggs for 15 minutes using the whisk attachment.  Add the sugar a little at the time, then add the salt and vanilla.  Beat for 15 minutes more.  Don't shorten the time; the dough really needs to be beaten for the entire 30 minutes.

 

Stir together flour and baking powder and slowly add it to the mix.  As soon as the dough gets too heavy, change from the whisk to the dough hook.   Stir in the butter and honey.

 

Let the dough rest for 30 minutes, then turn it onto a well-floured board and knead into a smooth ball.  Put the dough into a plastic bag and refrigerate for 2 hours.

 

Preheat the oven to 225° F.  Remove 1/4 of the dough, pat it out into a floured board, and roll it with a Springerle roller to a 1/4" thickness.  Sprinkle the cookie sheets with anise seeds and place the cut cookies on top of the seeds.

 

Bake for 45 minutes, but do not let the cookies turn brown.  They will not be hard and can be eaten when cool.

 

I found it easier to pat out squares of dough and press them deeply into the molds, then carefully remove the dough.  The anise seeds on the cookie sheets will bake small holes in the back of the cookie.
