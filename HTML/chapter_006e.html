ANCHOIADE

Anchovy and Garlic Purée

Anchoïade is a salty, creamy little thirst-stimulator that works its magic perfectly alongside tapenade and its accompaniments at aperitif time. I use the anchovies preserved in oil rather than in salt as is traditional. If eating anchovies on their own, though, I’d plump for the salted, quicky rinsed and tarted up with lemon.

MAKES 300g (10½oz )
40 anchovy fillets in oil
1 large garlic clove, peeled
75ml (2½ fl oz) olive oil
2 large sprigs savory or thyme, leaves picked from the stalks

Blend all of the ingredients together to the texture that you prefer, either chunky or fine.

Eat immediately or store in the fridge, covered with more oil for up to a month.
