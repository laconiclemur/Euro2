Streusel Tarts with Lemon Curd

 

Can't help yourself sneaking streusel from an inconspicuous place on a cake because it tastes so delicious?  Gaylee gets caught almost every time.  Then she decided to bake these little tarts.  Streusel without guilt.  Perfect little treats for a lady's coffee.  Best not to keep them too long, as if that is even possible.

 

Thelma from Australia contributed the recipe for the lemon curd.

 

For the lemon curd:

·         1/2 stick of butter

·         1/4 cup sugar, or a little more if you want it sweeter

·         2 eggs, well-beaten

·         juice and grated rind of a large lemon

·         1 1/2 tsp. lemon extract

 

For the streusel:

·         1/3 cup plus 1 Tbsp. flour

·         1/3 cup sugar

·         1/2 stick unsalted butter

 

For the bottoms:

·         1 1/4 cup all-purpose flour

·         1 tsp. baking powder

·         1/2 cup sugar

·         6 Tbsps. unsalted butter

·         1 egg, lightly beaten

·         1 tsp. vanilla

 

Make the lemon curd:

Combine everything in a small pot of a double-boiler.  Stir over boiling water until it is thick.  Cool in the refrigerator.  You can also use apricot jam instead of the lemon curd.

 

Make the streusel:      

Combine the flour and sugar in a small bowl.  Melt the butter in a medium pan.  Remove from heat and stir in the flour mix.  The mixture will form several clumps.  Place in the fridge until cold.

 

Make the dough:

To make the dough by hand, mix the dry ingredients.  Cut in the butter with a pastry cutter.  Add the lightly beaten egg and vanilla.  Knead until the dough is smooth.

 

To make the dough in a food processor, pulse the dry ingredients.  Add the butter and pulse a few times until coarse crumbles form.  Add the slightly beaten egg with the vanilla and process until the dough forms a ball.

 

Preheat the oven to 400° F.  Line a cookie sheet with parchment paper.  Divide the dough into about 24 pieces and place on the sheet.  With your fingers, flatten the dough into about 1 1/2 inch rounds.  Spread the rounds with lemon curd or apricot jam.  Crumble the streusel in clumps on top of the filling.  Bake 11 to 13 minutes.
