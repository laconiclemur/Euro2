The $250 Niemann Marcus Cookie

 

Madeleine dusted off her recipe archives and found an old mimeographed copy of this delicious cookie.  The recipe is strictly for curiosity, but it also is very tasty.  I have no idea whether this story is true, if it really came from the famous department store, or if the lady really paid $250 to obtain the recipe or if it was just a joke.  In any case, it is one of the "urban legends" and must be around since at least 1991.  The ingredients make a huge amount, so here I will just list half the recipe.  You will need a seriously hard working standing mixer and switch to the dough hook before the dough gets too stiff.

 

·         2 1/2 cups oats

·         1 - 6 oz. Hershey bar, broken into pieces, or 6 oz. chocolate chips

·         1/2 tsp. salt

·         1 tsp. baking soda

·         1 tsp. baking powder

·         2 sticks butter

·         1 cup sugar

·         1 cup brown sugar

·         2 eggs

·         1 tsp. vanilla

·         12 oz. semi-sweet chocolate chips

·         1 1/2 cups chopped nuts

 

Measure the oats and put it into a food processor or blender.  Add the Hershey bar and process both to a fine powder.  Mix together the ground oats, salt, baking soda, and baking powder and set aside.

 

Cream together butter and both sugars.  Mix in eggs and vanilla.  Gradually beat in the powdered oatmeal mix.  Stir in the chocolate chips and the chopped nuts.

 

Preheat the oven to 375° F.  Line a cookie sheet with parchment paper.  Pinch off pieces of dough, roll by hand into about 1-1/2" balls and place on cookie sheet about 2" apart.  Bake 10 minutes.

 

This makes about 70 cookies.
