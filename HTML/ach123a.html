potato salad

insalata di patate

Potatoes prepared this way make a great lunch dish served along with roasted peppers, a green salad, and an assortment of olives and cheeses. I also serve this salad with grilled meat or as part of a buffet. Serve it warm, at room temperature, or chilled.

6 large all-purpose, red, or Yukon Gold potatoes (about 2 pounds), scrubbed

1 clove garlic, crushed in a press

1 teaspoon Dijon mustard

2 tablespoons red wine vinegar

Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper

1/4 cup olive oil

1 tablespoon chopped fresh Italian, flat leafed parsley

2 tablespoons chopped onions or shallots

1. Place the potatoes in a large pot. Cover with water to about 1 inch above the potatoes. Add salt and bring to a boil. Continue to boil until tender when pierced with a knife, about 15 minutes. Drain, run under cold water to cool and cut into 2-inch pieces. (If using all-purpose potatoes, peel and discard the skins.) Place in a large serving bowl.

2. In a small bowl, whisk the garlic, mustard, vinegar, and salt and pepper to taste. Gradually whisk in the olive oil. Pour over the potatoes and toss to coat evenly. Add the parsley and onions, toss gently, and serve.

— SERVES 4 —

WINE PAIRING: Sparkling and rosé
