Old-Fashioned Mincemeat Cookies

 

The traveling sisters, Florence and Millie, gave me this recipe.  It came originally from a NoneSuch mincemeat can, and their mother was baking these cookies as early as the 1930’s.

 

·         1 cup shortening

·         2 cups sugar

·         3 eggs, well beaten

·         2 Tbsps. milk

·         4 to 4 1/2 cups cake flour

·         4 tsps. baking powder

·         1/2 tsp. salt

·         Mincemeat, either original or rum and brandy, enough to fill the cookies.

 

Cream the shortening and sugar.  Mix in eggs and milk.  In a separate bowl, combine flour, baking powder and salt.  Gradually beat the flour into the shortening mix.  Chill the dough; you can even let it rest overnight in the fridge.

 

Preheat the oven to 390° F.  Line a cookie sheet with parchment paper.  On a floured board, roll out the dough about 1/4 inch thick and cut rounds with a biscuit cutter.  Put about a tablespoon or less of mincemeat in the middle, cover with another round and crimp the edges closed. Bake 10 minutes.  Dust with powdered sugar when done.

 

If your cutter measures 2 inches, the cookies will grow to 3 inches when baked and make about 55 cookies.

 

Note - Cake flour has a lower protein content and yields softer cakes and here a more tender cookie.  You can substitute the cake flour by subtracting 2 Tablespoons flour from each cup and adding 2 Tablespoon cornstarch back in.  For the amount called for here, reduce it by 9 Tablespoons flour and add in 9 Tablespoons cornstarch.
