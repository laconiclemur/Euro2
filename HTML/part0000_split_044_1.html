Week 36: I’m Thirsty!

Review: Week 33: I’m Hungry!

Vocabulary:

Tu as soif ? - (Are you thirsty?)

oo-ah-swahf

J’ai soif. - (I’m thirsty.)

jhee-swahf

Grammar note: The phrase, “Tu as soif?” literally means “Do you have thirst?”. Similarly, “J’ai soif.” means, “I have thirst.” This is yet another example of why we translate for meaning, not word for word.

Cultural note: Coco Chanel was born very poor in 1883, grew up in an orphanage and was taught to sew. She took those skills and became the most influential fashion designer of the twentieth century. Her business empire lasts to this day, still including her signature perfume, suits and little black dress.

Idea! A hot summer’s day is a great time to make a pitcher of lemonade and see who is thirsty. Weather not warm? Try and make some hot cocoa instead!
