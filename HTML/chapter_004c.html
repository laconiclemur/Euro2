Sautéed Wild Mushrooms

As simple and short as it is, a recipe is definitely merited. The heating of the oil until it is smoking and the addition of the butter that cooks to a foamy nuttiness are so, so vital. To my mind and taste, the ideal mushrooms for this are either sanguines (saffron milk caps) or ceps, but failing either of these you could use large cap mushrooms which tend to have a fairly gutsy, if not wild, flavour. I like to use flat-leaf parsley as opposed to the more common curly. Rather than chop it madly, leaving you with a green board and a bunch of wet stuff that smells like freshly mown grass, I slice it finely, which you can either do with a sharp knife or a pair of scissors.

FOR 4
Around 600g (1lb 5oz) wild mushrooms
3 tbsp olive oil
50g (1¾ oz) unsalted butter
3 garlic cloves, peeled and finely chopped
1 small bunch flat-leaf parsley, leaves picked from the stalks, sliced
Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Preheat your oven to 180°C/350°F/Gas 4.

Heat a large heavy frying pan with the olive oil until it is smoking, then add the mushrooms in a single layer and just leave them for a couple of minutes to let the heat get back into the pan. Toss them a little, then fry them for a further few minutes until they are well coloured. Next add the butter and let this cook until it is golden brown and has a healthy smell of nuts. Sprinkle over the garlic and parsley and as soon as you are hit by their aroma, season the mushrooms and serve and eat them immediately, as a starter or accompaniment.
