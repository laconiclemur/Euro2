I love to cook and eat fish and enjoy everything from the very simplest dishes to more elaborate recipes. I grew up eating fish on Fridays, and this Catholic tradition still prevails in much of rural France. Even if you’re not religious, this is an excellent custom to keep up – and healthy too. Good-quality fish is available all over France, often in local markets. In central France they probably eat more freshwater species, while in Brittany and Normandy shellfish are the stars of many recipes. Salt cod is particularly popular in southern France. Good farmed fish, such as salmon, bream, bass, turbot and trout, is also available now at reasonable prices.
