Spritz Cookies

 

This is a very traditional German cookie and one of the best.  The name again makes me wonder, “spritzen” means spraying, so that should imply an easy and light dough.  Instead, the dough is so stiff that it is normally pushed through a meat grinder fitted with a special decorative disk to make the typical grooved surface.  Elfriede bakes plenty of these cookies every year.

 

·         2 cups flour

·         1 1/3 cups cornstarch

·         1 1/3 cups sugar

·         2 packages Dr. Oetker vanilla sugar

·         pinch of salt

·         3 sticks butter

·         1 1/4 cup finely ground almonds

·         melted chocolate chips for dipping the cookies

 

Mix together flour and cornstarch.  Add the sugar, vanilla sugar and salt.  Cut in the butter and almonds.  Knead into a dough.

 

If you own a meat grinder or spritz cookie press, use it to press the dough and make shapes.  Otherwise, it is easiest to roll pieces of dough between your hands, flatten them out and make decorative patterns with a fork.

 

Preheat the oven to 350° F.  Place cookies on a lined cookie sheet.  Bake for 10 minutes.  They stay fairly white.  Remove cookies and let cool completely.

 

When cool, dip half of each cookie into melted chocolate.
