Far Breton
(Far Breton)

Preparation time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 45 minutes to 1 hour

Servings: 8

A far is a custard pudding cake, similar to a clafouti but with a dense, smooth, flan-like texture. The authentic Far Breton recipe dates back to the 18th century and originated in Brittany (Bretagne), a region in the northwest of France. Both refined and easy to make, this sweet specialty is representative of Brittany’s gastronomy. This special variation with soaked prunes can be served for breakfast or as a dessert.

Ingredients

2 cups of whole milk

3 large eggs

½ cup of sugar

5 Tbsp. unsalted butter, melted and cooled

¼ tsp. vanilla

⅛ tsp. salt

¼ cup of flour

1 cup of small to medium size pitted prunes

¼ cup of hot water

¼ cup of Armagnac, rum, or French Brandy

Powdered sugar

 

1. Pre-soak the prunes in a mixture of the water and alcohol in a sealed jar overnight or up to 24 hours.

2. Combine the flour, sugar, and salt in a bowl and mix. In a separate bowl, mix the milk, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla. Pour the liquid mixture into the dry one and mix well until smooth. Let the batter rest at room temperature for 30 minutes to an hour.

3. Preheat the oven to 375 F. Butter and then dust the interior of a shallow baking dish with flour.

4. Stir the batter and pour into the dish. Drop the prunes in the batter so that they are evenly distributed and drizzle the rest of the liquor syrup over the top. Bake for 45 minutes to 1 hour or until golden and an inserted knife comes out clean. Cool completely before unmolding. Dust the top with powdered sugar and serve.
