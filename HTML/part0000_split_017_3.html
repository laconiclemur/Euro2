Coconut Kisses

 

These are Dad's absolute favorites.  We bake several batches every year, but learned a long time ago to bake only one batch at the time.  Since I only have an electric hand mixer, making more than one recipe at a time will make it burn up.

 

 

For the glaze:

·         4 1/2 cups powdered sugar, roughly

·         1/2 cup water

 

For the cookies:

·         7 oz. sweetened coconut flakes, 1 bag

·         2 1/4 cups flour

·         1/2 tsp. baking soda

·         2 sticks butter, at room temperature

·         1 cup sugar

·         2 eggs

·         1 Tbsp. vanilla

 

To make the glaze, add the water to the powdered sugar very slowly and stir until it is a thin liquid.  Have the glaze ready when the cookies come from the oven.

 

Preheat the oven to 350° F.  Spread the coconut flakes on a cookie sheet and very carefully toast until the coconut is lightly and evenly browned.  Let cool.

 

Mix the flour and baking soda in a small bowl.  In a large bowl, cream the butter and sugar, then blend in the eggs and vanilla.  Slowly add the flour mix and then the toasted coconut flakes.

 

Preheat the oven to 350° F.  Line cookie sheets with parchment.  Drop the dough by tablespoons onto the cookie sheets and bake for 10 minutes until slightly golden.

 

While still very warm, drop the cookies into the glaze.  Place them on wax paper until they are completely dry and cooled.
