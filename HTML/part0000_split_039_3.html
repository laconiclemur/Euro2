Dresdner Heidesand

 

These cookies have a very rich buttery flavor but fall apart like sand in your mouth.  They are a little challenging to bake.

 

·         2 cups flour

·         1 1/3 cup sugar

·         1 package Dr. Oetker Vanilla sugar

·         2 1/2 sticks butter

·         sugar for sprinkling

 

Mix the flour, sugar and vanilla sugar in a bowl.  Melt the butter until it begins to brown, taking care not to burn it.  Remove from the heat and let cool for a little.  Add the flour and mix, incorporating the ingredients well.

 

Preheat the oven to 360° F.  Line a cookie sheet with parchment paper.

 

On wax paper or plastic wrap, roll the dough into several sausages, each about as thick as a quarter.  Wrap the rolls in plastic wrap and cool in the fridge until they can be sliced into about 1/2 inch thick discs.  It can be difficult to find the best temperature for the roll to slice without crumbling it.  If too cold, it may be too hard to slice, so let it warm up again if necessary.

 

Sprinkle with sugar.  Bake 25 minutes until the cookies start to look golden.  Let cool.
