Oma Minna's Pfeffernüsse

 

Oma Minna was born in 1867 in northern Germany, and her recipe is still enjoyed every year by her family.  It is best to start baking 1 month before the Pfeffernüsse are needed, to develop the best flavor.  Because they use quite a lot of room, the cookies are now air dried on a repurposed flat screen door.  This recipe makes many, many, many.

 

For the cookies:

·         8 cups all-purpose flour

·         2 cups sugar

·         3 Tbsp. each baking powder and soda

·         2 tsp. anise

·         1 tsp. each ground cloves, nutmeg and cinnamon

·         1 cup vegetable oil

·         2 cups honey

·         3 eggs, slightly beaten

·         2 Tbsp. instant coffee granules, dissolved in a tiny bit of hot water

 

For the icing:

·         2 cups liquid whipping cream

·         1-1/2 to 2 pounds powdered sugar

 

For the cookies, work all ingredients into a smooth dough.  Start in a huge bowl with the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda and spices.  Mix well.  Add vegetable oil, honey, eggs and dissolved coffee granules.  Stir until combined and then knead with hands until smooth.

 

Preheat the oven to 350° F.  Line a cookie sheet with parchment paper.  With a teaspoon, make hazelnut-sized small balls of dough.  Place balls on parchment paper.  Bake for 10 minutes.

 

For the icing, mix the whipping cream with the powdered sugar.  Let the cookies cool completely, then ice.  Let them air dry 2 to 3 days on the screen door.
