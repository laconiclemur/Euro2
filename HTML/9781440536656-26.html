Middle Eastern Tabbouleh

Since you can serve this chilled or at room temperature, it’s a good make-ahead candidate. The fresh yet hearty quality to this salad gives it a balanced feel.

Serves 6

Ingredients

1⁄2 cup medium bulgur wheat

11⁄2 cups water

1⁄3 cup lemon juice

2 tablespoons chopped fresh mint

1 teaspoon salt

1 teaspoon pepper

1⁄4 cup extra-virgin olive oil

1 cup chopped fresh parsley

1⁄2 cup chopped green onions

2 large tomatoes, diced

1⁄2 cup diced cucumber

1 tablespoon minced garlic

  1. Soak the bulgur in the water for at least 2 hours.

  2. Drain the excess water and put the bulgur in a large bowl.

  3. Add the remaining ingredients to the bulgur and mix well.

  4. Let sit at room temperature for 1 hour or refrigerate overnight.

  5. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

Make a Swap

You may use couscous instead of bulgur wheat for tabbouleh. Couscous is fluffier and has finer texture than bulgur and is faster to make.
