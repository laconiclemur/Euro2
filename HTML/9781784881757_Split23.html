fresh tomato passata

This is Andrea Falcone’s method of making a passata to use in soups such as the Winter Tomato & Bread Soup. He includes the skins as this increases the flavour of the soup. You can also use it in place of tinned tomatoes when making a tomato sauce (see opposite).

MAKES APPROX. 750 ML (25 FL OZ/3 CUPS)

1 kg (2 lb 3 oz) fresh ripe, flavourful tomatoes

salt and freshly ground black pepper

Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F/Gas 6). Cut the tomatoes in half (around the equator, not pole to pole) and put them cut side up in a roasting tray. Sprinkle with salt and pepper and roast for 20 minutes or until they just start to collapse and brown. Remove from the oven and put the tomatoes in a blender or food processor. Pulse to blend, until smooth. The passata will keep for up to 4 days in the fridge.
