The Open House Chocolate Cookie

 

For the true chocoholic like Gaylee, this 4 bite cookie is filled with plenty of chocolate chips and has just enough dough around it so your fingers won't get dirty.  A little crispy on the outside and soft inside and a bit of hazelnut make for an interesting texture.

 

·         1/2 cup coarsely chopped hazelnuts

·         1 1/4 cup flour

·         1 tsp. baking powder

·         1/2 cup sugar

·         6 Tbsps. butter

·         1 egg, lightly beaten

·         1 Tbsp. vanilla

·         1 1/2 to 2 cups semi sweet chocolate chips - just use the whole 12 oz. bag

 

Put the hazelnuts into a plastic bag and whack them a couple of times with a rolling pin or the flat side of a meat mallet, just enough to break them up.  Set aside.

 

In the food processor, pulse the flour, baking powder and sugar a few times to mix.  Cut in the butter and process until crumbly.  Add the lightly beaten eggs and vanilla and process until a dough ball forms.  By hand, work in the nuts and chocolate chips.

 

Preheat the oven to 350° F.  Line a cookie sheet with parchment paper.  Form tablespoon-sized balls and set them on the cookie sheet.  Check where the most chocolate chips are visible and flatten the tops ever so slightly.  Bake 11 to 13 minutes.
