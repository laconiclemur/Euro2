kate’s banana bread

This recipe is from my late wife, Kate, who passed away in 2009. Not only was she an extraordinary person, she was also a great cook and a wonderful baker. Making this recipe is just one of the ways we keep her close to us.

Kate’s banana bread was devoured weekly by our kids and by the endless stream of their friends who came through our doors over the years. Serve warm with a dollop of butter.

11/4 cups all-purpose flour

1 teaspoon baking soda

1/2 cup (1 stick) butter, softened

1/2 cup sugar

2 large eggs

1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

3 ripe bananas

1/4 cup Grape-Nuts cereal

1. Preheat the oven to 350° F. Grease a 4 x 8-inch loaf pan and line with parchment paper.

2. In a medium-size bowl, sift the flour and baking soda. In another medium-size bowl, use a wooden spoon or whisk to mix together the butter and sugar. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating after each addition. Add the vanilla and the bananas, mashing them into the batter. Stir in the Grape-Nuts.

3. Spoon the batter into the prepared loaf pan. Bake until golden brown on top and a cake tester comes out clean, about 45 minutes.
