The Traditional German Cinnamon Star

 

These are one of the most typically traditional German cookies.  Every German Oma has a recipe for them.  They use no flour and are quite crunchy.

 

For the cookies:

·         3 egg whites

·         1/2 tsp. cream of tartar

·         2 1/3 cups powdered sugar

·         1 1/2 tsps. cinnamon

·         grated peel of 1 fresh lemon

·         3 cups ground almonds, preferably unpeeled almonds

·         a little Dr. Oetker Arrack extract, optional

 

For the glaze:

·         1 Tbsp. lemon juice

·         2 1/2 cups powdered sugar

 

Beat the egg whites with cream of tartar and slowly add the powdered sugar, cinnamon and lemon peel.  Beat for about 20 minutes, then fold in the ground almonds.

 

Dust the work surface with powdered sugar, roll out the dough and cut out stars.

 

Preheat the oven to 350° F.  Place stars on a prepared cookie sheet and bake about 10 minutes, or until they start to look lightly golden.

 

To frost, stir lemon juice into powdered sugar until you have a spreadable consistency.  Adjust the amount of liquid to get a consistency you like.  Frost the cookies.  When the glaze is dry, store the cookies in tins.
