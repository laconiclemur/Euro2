Doctor's Kisses

 

This is from the same 1899 cookbook.  Nowhere in it did I find baking powder or baking soda.  But I did find other terms I never heard, like “spinning sugar or spreading a baking sheet with wax.”  Spinning sugar (I assume) was either caramelizing the sugar or turning it into syrup.  Wax?  What kind of wax would stand up to baking, even in a "cool " oven?  And what about the name - Doctor's Kisses?  Healing anything that ailed you?  Maybe.  Anyway, I always feel better with cookies.

 

·         2 eggs

·         1 2/3 cups sugar

·         2 cups flour, scant

·         candied lemon peel, finely sliced

·         sliced almonds

 

Cream the eggs with sugar until the mix starts to get thick.  Stir in the flour.  Cover a baking sheet with wax.  (I’d suggest using parchment paper instead.)  Drop small mounds on it.  Sprinkle with the lemon peel and sliced almonds.

 

Let the cookies rest for an hour and bake in a “not too hot” oven.  Sorry, that’s all the directions the original recipe gave!  In those times, there were only wood fired stoves and the cook in the house would know what this meant.  To gauge the temperature in the oven,the cook put a piece of paper in the oven.  If the paper turned yellow slowly, then you had the right temperature for baking almost everything.  Turned the paper brown in a hurry, it was too hot.  I suggest trying 350° F until lightly golden.
