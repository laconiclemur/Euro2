Andy's Sandwich Cookies

 

A very simple dough for cut out cookies.  What makes it special is the filling and the glaze.  One recipe does not make very many cookies, so I always make several batches.

 

For the cookies:

·         3 cups all-purpose flour

·         1/2 tsp. baking powder

·         1 1/3 cups sugar

·         2 sticks unsalted butter

·         1 Tbsp. fresh or dried lemon peel

·         3 egg yolks

·         1 egg, lightly beaten with the extra yolks

·         your favorite jam, such as raspberry, currant, apricot, or strawberry

 

For the lemon glaze:

·         about 2 cups powdered sugar

·         a little lemon juice

 

In a food processor, pulse flour and baking powder.  Add sugar and pulse again to mix.  Add the butter and lemon peel and pulse until it looks crumbly.  Add the egg yolks and lightly beaten eggs and process until a ball forms.

 

Preheat the oven to 350° F.  Line a cookie sheet with parchment paper.  Roll out the dough to about 1/4 inch thick, cut out circles and place them on the cookie sheet.  Bake 6 to 8 minutes, until the cookies start to turn golden.  Let them cool completely.

 

When cold, spread the back of one cookie with your favorite jam and cover with a second cookie.

 

To make the glaze, stir a few drops of lemon juice into the powdered sugar.  Continue to add a few drops at a time until the consistency is thick, but still liquid.  Frost the tops of the sandwich cookies thickly with the glaze.  Add sprinkles if you like and let the cookies dry completely before stacking in a tin.
