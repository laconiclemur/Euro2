Molasses Ginger Cookies

 

Although not a gingerbread, it tastes a little like gingerbread, so that is why I included them in this group.  This is a very old recipe, and it came from Mrs. Owens via Pat.  Mrs. Owens died in 1975 at age 100, and the molasses cookies were her mother's recipe.  It dates back to 1840 before commercial baking powder was available.  Back then, people assumed everyone knew how to bake and directions were not necessary.  Well, we like instructions nowadays.

 

·         1 cup sugar

·         1 cup shortening or 2 sticks butter, softened but still firm

·         2 eggs

·         1 cup molasses

·         1 tsp. each salt, cinnamon, ground cloves and ginger

·         about 6 cups flour, enough to make a soft dough

·         2 1/2 tsp. soda, dissolved in 1/2 cup cold coffee

 

Cream the sugar and shortening thoroughly.  Add eggs and molasses.  Then mix in the spices and salt with the flour, alternating with the coffee.  Work until you have a soft dough.

 

Preheat the oven to 350° F.  Roll out about 1/4" thick and cut out large cookies, or shape walnut size balls.  Bake for 12 minutes, and remember it is better to under-bake these.  When the cookies are cool, they will firm up.
