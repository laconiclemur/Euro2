Elisen Lebkuchen from Dresden

 

My nana “Daddy" used to bake this gingerbread for us in East Germany.  There were not too many ingredients required, but even these few things were frequently impossible to obtain.

 

·         2 egg whites

·         1 1/2 cups ground hazelnuts

·         1 cup sugar

·         1 package Dr. Oetker Vanilla sugar

·         a very scant 1/2 cup all-purpose flour

·         1 tsp. baking powder

·         1/4 cup candied lemon peel, very finely chopped

 

Preheat the oven to 350° F.  Beat the egg whites until stiff.  Fold in the rest of the ingredients.  Spread the dough on baking wafers, or it works just fine on parchment also.  Shape the Elisen into 2 inch rounds.  They will spread to about 3 1/2 inches.  Bake 18 minutes or until the edges turn lightly golden.  Makes 12 gingerbreads.

 

Glaze as in the recipe above.

 

Andy's version:  Add 1/4 cup dry little blueberries while folding in the rest of the ingredients.
