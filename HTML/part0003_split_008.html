Poireaux vinaigrette aux oeufs

Leek salad egg vinaigrette

Make this simple French classic with young tender leeks for a perfect lunch or starter.

Serves 4

16 young leeks, washed

2 free-range eggs, hard-boiled and chopped

1 bunch of chives, snipped

salt

black pepper

vinaigrette

2 tbsp Dijon mustard

2 tbsp red wine vinegar

4–6 tbsp water

300ml vegetable or peanut oil

 

Trim off the dark green tops of the leeks, leaving about 3cm of the light green. Bring a large pan of salted water to the boil, add the leeks and cook them until tender – this should take about 6 minutes. Drain the leeks and lay them flat on a rack to cool slightly while you make the vinaigrette.

Put the mustard, vinegar and seasoning in a bowl with a little of the water. Whisk as if making a mayonnaise, slowly adding the oil to emulsify the vinaigrette. Add a little more water if the mixture becomes too thick.

Cut the leeks in half lengthways and arrange them on a dish or plate. Scatter over the chopped egg and snipped chives. Drizzle with the vinaigrette and eat while the leeks are still warm.
