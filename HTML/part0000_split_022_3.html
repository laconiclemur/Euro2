Biscochitos

 

Leila's mom is a Native American.  She made these incredibly tender cookies for a long time for her family in Espanola.  This little gem is also the official cookie of the great state of New Mexico.

 

If you have a standing mixer, haul it out.  The hand mixer did well when I just made 1/2 of the recipe.

 

·         6 cups flour

·         5 tsps. baking powder

·         1/4 tsp. baking soda

·         2 cups lard (can be found in a small blue box in a regular super market)

·         1 1/4 cup sugar

·         2 eggs

·         1 tsp. vanilla

·         about 2 Tbsps. Anise seeds, semi-crushed

·         4 Tbsps. water

·         cinnamon and sugar, mixed together for sprinkling on top

 

Sift together flour, baking powder and baking soda.  In a separate bowl, cream lard and sugar.  Mix in eggs, vanilla and anise.  Add flour mixture, alternating with water, and mix well.

 

Preheat the oven to 350° F.  Line cookie sheet with parchment paper.  Roll the dough out on parchment about 1/4" thick.  Cut out shapes, place on the prepared cookie sheets.  Sprinkle liberally with cinnamon sugar.  Bake about 10 minutes, until the cookies are still blond.
