VIN D’ORANGE

Orange Wine

This extremely popular aperitif is traditionally made with a mixture of sweet and bitter oranges, but it is lovely made with either alone, or with blood oranges or even lemons. I’ve given fairly large quantities to make it worthwhile, as it is best left for several weeks to mature. Serve very cold in small wine glasses.

MAKES ABOUT 2.5–3 LITRES
5 oranges
1 vanilla pod, split and scraped
500g (1lb 2oz) caster sugar
3 bottles rosé or white wine
500ml (18fl oz) eau-de-vie or vodka

Wash then halve the oranges and slice them finely. Mix with the vanilla pod and sugar in a large non-reactive container with a lid, then pour over the wine and eau-de-vie. Stir well and cover, then keep in a cool dark place for four to six weeks, stirring from time to time. Strain through a muslin and bottle.
