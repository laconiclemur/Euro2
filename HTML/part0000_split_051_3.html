Pfeffernüsse

 

This recipe was given to me in 1959 by my nana "Daddy" in Saxony, which was in the former East Germany.  That was the year my family left the communist regime for a much better life in the West, and I was afraid I would never see her again.  Luckily, she was able to flee a year later via West Berlin and lived with us in Hof / Bavaria until her death 1980.

 

For the gingerbread:

·         2 eggs

·         1 3/4 cup sugar

·         1 1/2 cup all-purpose flour

·         scant 1/2 cup corn starch

·         1 tsp. each of cinnamon and baking powder

·         a pinch each of ground cloves, allspice, nutmeg and pepper

·         1/3 cup finely sliced candied lemon peel

·         1 Tbsp. rum

 

For the glaze:

·         2 egg whites

·         2/12 cup powdered sugar

·         1 to 2 Tbsp. rum

 

In a large bowl, beat the eggs and sugar until creamy.  In a separate bowl, sift together the flour, starch, baking powder and all spices.  Add the flour mixture to the egg mixture along with the candied lemon peel and the rum.  Knead into a soft dough.  Let it rest for 1 hour.

 

Preheat the oven to 400° F.  Place parchment paper on cookie sheet.  Roll out dough to 1/2 inch thickness and cut out small, round cookies.  Bake for 12 minutes.

 

To make the glaze, beat the egg whites until foamy.  Add the powdered sugar and the rum.  The glaze needs to be thick.  Cover the cookies with the glaze while still hot.
