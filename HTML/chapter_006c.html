SAUCE TOMATE

Cooked Tomato Sauce

In any Mediterranean country where there’s enough sun to ripen the tomatoes on the vine, there’s always going to be an abundance of tomato sauces made. In Provence, the flavour is intensified and sweetened even more by the addition of dried orange peel. Simple as it sounds, the difference it makes has to be tried to be believed. In Provence, the sauce enhances anything from grilled aubergines through pasta and, of course, the famous Petits Farcis Provençaux (see here).

MAKES ABOUT 800g (1lb 12oz), ENOUGH FOR PASTA FOR 4
1 large onion, peeled and chopped
3 strips Dried Orange Zest (see here)
4 garlic cloves, peeled and chopped
4 tbsp olive oil
600g (1lb 5oz) very ripe tomatoes, or 2 × 400g (14oz) tins chopped tomatoes
2 tbsp tomato concentrate
Salt, cayenne pepper and soft brown sugar

Sweat the chopped onion, orange zest and garlic in the olive oil over a low heat for 5 minutes until soft and transparent. While they are cooking, core and chop the tomatoes.

Add the tomato and tomato concentrate along with about 100ml (3½fl oz) water to the pan and simmer for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add more water if necessary. Purée in a blender then strain through a fine sieve. Season well with salt, cayenne and brown sugar.
