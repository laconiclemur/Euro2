Veggie Empanadas

Empanadas are a popular small bite in most of Latin America, although they are traditionally stuffed with a spicy meat filling. This version uses a vegetable filling, but you can also get creative!

Serves 6

Ingredients

2 rounds pie dough

1⁄2 cup diced onion

1⁄2 cup corn kernels

1⁄4 cup diced roasted red bell peppers

1 tablespoon seasoning salt

1 egg, beaten with 1 tablespoon water

  1. Cut 3-inch circles out of the pie dough. Preheat oven to 350°F.

  2. Combine the onion, corn, peppers, and seasoning salt. Put a spoonful of the mixture in the middle of each dough circle.

  3. Brush the edges of the dough circles with beaten egg, fold the dough over to make a half circle, and press the edges to seal. Use a fork to crimp the edges.

  4. Place the empanadas on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and brush the tops with the beaten egg.

  5. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes, until the tops are golden. Serve warm.

Have Them for Dessert, Too!

Various fruit fillings, such as cherry, apricot, raspberry, and peach, can be used to make dessert empanadas. After they are baked, dust them with powdered sugar for a great tapas dessert.
