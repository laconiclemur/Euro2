white sauce

besciamella

This recipe makes a medium-consistency besciamella (béchamel) sauce, which may be used in many recipes in place of cream. It can be prepared a few hours ahead of time and kept at room temperature. Place a piece of plastic wrap or waxed paper directly on the surface of the sauce to prevent a skin from forming.

6 tablespoons (3/4 stick) butter

6 tablespoons all-purpose flour

3 cups whole or skim milk, warmed

Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper

1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg

Melt the butter in a medium-size saucepan set over medium-low heat. When the butter is foaming, whisk in the flour. Whisk in the milk. Cook, whisking frequently, until the sauce thickens, about 20 minutes. Remove from the heat and stir in salt and pepper to taste and the nutmeg.

— SERVES 4 (MAKES ABOUT 3 CUPS) —

WINE PAIRING: Full white

NOTE: This recipe may be easily cut in half or in thirds when smaller amounts are called for in various recipes throughout this book. Smaller quantities will require less cooking time.
