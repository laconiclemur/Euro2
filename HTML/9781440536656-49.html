White Sangria

A lighter take on red sangria, this can be given a shot in the arm by using sparkling wine in place of the white wine. It’s perfect for entertaining or for sipping on a hot summer day!

Seves 4

Ingredients

3 cups dry, light white table wine

1⁄2 cup brandy (optional)

1⁄4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice

1⁄4 cup freshly squeezed lime juice

1⁄4 cup freshly squeezed orange juice

1⁄4 cup sugar (or more to taste)

1 cup seedless green grapes

1 orange

1 lemon

1 lime

1 peach

  1. Combine the white wine, brandy, lemon juice, lime juice, orange juice, and sugar in a pitcher. Stir well and add sufficient ice to chill the mixture without all melting.

  2. Cut the green grapes in half and add them to the pitcher.

  3. Slice the orange, lemon, and lime into 1⁄4-inch-thick slices and add them to the pitcher.

  4. Cut the peach in half, remove the pit, and cut each half into wedges and add them to the pitcher.

  5. Pour a glass for each person and be sure to include a piece of each fruit in each serving.
