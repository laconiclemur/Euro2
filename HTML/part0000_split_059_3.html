Hazelnut Torte

 

This torte is for a special occasion, but is actually easy to make.  It would be best to have two 10" spring form pans, with removable rims.  If you use only one form, bake the cake a little longer and test the middle until the toothpick comes out clean.  When the cake is cold and firm, carefully slice it horizontally once or twice.  Start the day before you like to serve the torte.

 

For the chocolate filling layer:

·         2 cups liquid whipping cream

·         1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips

 

For the torte:

·         8 egg whites

·         1 tsp. cream of tartar

·         3 1/4 cups powdered sugar

·         3 1/2 cups finely ground hazelnuts

 

For filling the torte:

·         1 jar Lingonberries preserves or 1 can whole cranberry sauce, divided

 

The day before, make the chocolate filling layer.  In a deep, narrow pan, gently heat the whipping cream.  Stir in the chocolate chips until everything is melted and smooth.  Refrigerate over night.

 

Preheat the oven to 350° F.  Line the bottom of the spring form pans with parchment, extending the paper over the edge.  Click the rims shut.  Spray the cake forms on the paper and the inside of the rims.

 

In a large bowl, beat the egg whites with the cream of tartar until stiff.  Sift the powdered sugar over the egg whites and gently fold in the hazelnuts.

 

Divide the dough into the two spring form pans and bake 30 minutes or until a tooth pick in the middle comes out clean.  Let cool completely.

 

Beat the chocolate whipping cream until stiff.  If you prefer it to be not so sweet, add a little cocoa powder to the whipping cream.

 

Spread a little dab of whipping cream mixture in the center of a cake platter.  This will help to keep the torte in place.  Place one of the cakes upside down (top down) on top of the whipping cream.  Spread the lingonberries or cranberry sauce liberally on the cake.  Spread some of the whipping cream mixture over the fruit filling.  Place the second cake over the filling, with the top side up.  Spread a little more fruit on top.  Frost the top and sides of the torte with the chocolate mixture.  Decorate with any remaining fruit.

 

Enjoy and wait for the compliments!!
