Raisin Cookies from Hof

 

Many service men probably remember a nice little town in northern Bavaria called Hof.  Air Force and Army personnel lived in the "Kaserne" and patrolled the border to the former East Germany.  Many "Hofer Mädla" now live in America with their sweethearts.  Marianne sent me this recipe from Hof.  This is a cake type cookie.

 

For the cookies:

·         8 egg whites

·         1/2 tsp. cream of tartar

·         2 1/2 sticks butter

·         1 1/3 cups sugar

·         2 Tbsps. grated lemon peel

·         8 egg yolks

·         2 Tbsps. rum

·         3 1/2 cups flour

·         2 tsps. baking powder

·         2 1/4 cups raisins (one 10 oz. box)

·         2 cups chopped walnuts

 

For the "Punschglasur":

·         2 1/2 cups powdered sugar

·         2 Tbsps. lemon juice

·         6 Tbsps. rum, or rum extract to taste

·         3 drops of red food color to give it a pinkish color

 

Beat the egg whites with the cream of tartar until stiff.  Set aside.

 

In a large bowl, beat the butter, sugar and lemon peel until fluffy.  Mix in the egg yolks and rum.  In a separate bowl, combine flour and baking powder, then slowly beat flour into the butter mixture.  Stir in the raisins and walnuts.  Fold the stiff egg whites into the dough.

 

Preheat the oven to 400° F.  Line cookie sheets with parchment paper.  Drop the cookie dough by small spoonfuls on the cookie sheets.  Bake 15 minutes.

 

For the Punschglasur, combine powdered sugar, lemon juice and rum, and food coloring if desired.  If using rum extract, you’ll need to add a little water to make it thinner.  Glaze the cookies.  For drizzling, a thicker glaze is better.  Spoon it into a plastic bag and cut off a tiny corner or use a pastry bag.

 

Another way to make a Punschglasur is mixing powdered sugar with red wine.
