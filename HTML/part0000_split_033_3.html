Stuffed Fresh Dates

 

This is from a cookbook which received the coveted “The Large Golden Medal with Golden Palm Tree" award in Vienna in 1899 and in Paris in 1900.  The book was meant for the higher class households who employed a cook. "The Austrian Cuisine" written by Marie von Rokitansky was a gift to my grandmother Angela in 1910 from her future in-laws.  That book also said that the cook had to wear two aprons, a clean one and a "working" apron underneath the clean one.  When the lady of the house called for the cook, she could remove the dirty one in a hurry and was presentable in her clean apron.  There are no amounts of ingredients suggested, so put it together how it tastes best to you.

 

Find the largest and best looking fresh dates, slice them length-wise and remove the seeds.  Use finely ground peeled almonds and add sugar to taste.  Add coarsely peeled pistachios, and mix in 1 egg white.  Stir everything well and stuff the dates with it.
