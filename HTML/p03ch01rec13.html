three ways to crush or mash garlic to a paste

1. It is common in Spain to pound garlic and a little salt in a mortar with a pestle and then mash it to a paste with a rotating motion.

2. A way popular with chefs here is to put the peeled cloves on a wooden board and roughly chop them with a large knife. Then add a pinch of coarse salt and crush the garlic with the flat side of the knife, holding it at a 45-degree angle and pressing down with force. Continue with a back-and-forth motion until you have a paste.

3. Use a garlic press. Some cooks don’t use them because they are difficult to wash—but it is easy with a nail brush.
