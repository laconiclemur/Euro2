Elisen Lebkuchen

 

I don't know if Ludwig van Beethoven had this gingerbread in mind when he composed "Für Elise," but without a doubt, Elisen are the queen of all Lebkuchen.  This recipe comes from Silvia in Oberfranken.

 

For the cookies:

·         2 eggs

·         3/4 cup sugar

·         a pinch salt

·         1/4 cup each candied lemon peel and orange peel, finely chopped

·         generous 1 1/2 cup ground almonds

·         1 cup ground hazelnuts

·         1 Tbsp. gingerbread spice mix

·         1/4 tsp. baking powder

·         large baking wafers, (Oblaten), for the bottom

 

For the glaze:

·         1 1/4 cup powdered sugar

·         3 Tbsp. lemon juice

 

Beat the eggs, sugar and salt until fluffy.  Add the candied peels, and then add the nuts, spice mix and baking powder and work into a dough.  Let rest over night.

 

Preheat the oven to 375° F.  With a spoon, spread the dough onto the baking wafers, leaving about a 5/8 inch edge all around.  (It works fine without the wafers, just shape the dough on parchment paper.)  Bake 15 to 20 minutes, until they turn slightly golden.

 

Mix together the powdered sugar and lemon juice for the glaze.  When the Elisen are cool, ice with a thin coating.
