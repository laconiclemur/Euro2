Unit 1: Making Friends

Week 1: Manners

Vocabulary:              

oui/non -               (yes/no)

wee/noh

s’il-vous plait -               (please)

sil voo play

merci - ( thanks)

mair-see

merci beaucoup - (thank you very much)

mair-see boh-ku

de rien - (you’re welcome)

duh- ree -ehn

Pronunciation note: The vowel sounds in French differ widely from the vowel sounds in English. Try to listen to spoken French to hear the differences.

Cultural Note: France is one of the largest countries in Europe. It has many beautiful areas: majestic mountain ranges, lovely countryside and beaches that are visited by tourists from around the world. Don’t forget..France also contains historic cities that are world centers of culture and business like Paris, Lyon and Marseille.

Idea! Use your new manners words at mealtimes. Pair the English word of whatever you want with the French manners word. Encourage your children to do the same. Don’t worry about mixing up the languages, that’s how communication is born.
