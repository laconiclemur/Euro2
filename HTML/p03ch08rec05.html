white beans with clams

{ alubias con almejas–cantabria }

Along the Cantabrian coast in the north of Spain, clams are large and meaty. You can make this dish with good-quality canned white beans. The beans acquire a delicate flavor from the wine and from the brine in the clams.

SERVES 2

1¼ pounds clams

3 tablespoons olive oil

1 large onion, chopped

3 garlic cloves, finely chopped

One 15-ounce can small white beans, drained

⅔ cup fruity white wine or cava

Salt

2 tablespoons chopped flat-leaf parsley

Wash the clams and throw away any that are not closed or that do not close when you rap them against the counter. Soak them in cold salted water for 1 hour, so that they release any sand they have inside.

Heat the oil in a wide casserole or pan with a tight-fitting lid. Put in the onion and stir over low heat until very soft and beginning to color, then add the garlic and stir for a minute or so. Add the beans, the wine, and a little salt, mix gently, and cook for 2 to 3 minutes.

Put the clams on top of the beans, put the lid on, and cook over medium-high heat for 3 to 5 minutes, until the clams open. Throw away any that do not open, and serve sprinkled with the parsley.

VARIATIONS

• Fry ½ small chile pepper, chopped, with the onion.

• Some cooks add a little pimentón (paprika) to the dish.
