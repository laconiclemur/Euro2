Apricot Cookies with Marzipan

 

This little cookie has marzipan as an ingredient, but not as a prevalent flavor.  If you have velvety dried apricots in your pantry, you can use them straight from the bag.  If the apricots are dry and hard, plump them in the microwave with a little orange juice, peach Schnapps or even Marillen Schnapps.  In Austria, apricots are called Marillen and the Schnapps is similar to Slivovitz, which, in this case, is made from plums.

 

·         6 Tbsps. butter

·         1/2 cup sugar

·         1 egg

·         a few drops almond extract, optional

·         1 1/3 cup flour

·         1 tsp. baking powder

·         1 cup diced dried apricots

·         6 oz. crumbled marzipan, or two 3 oz. logs with or without chocolate covering

 

Cream together the butter and sugar, then add the egg and the almond extract.  In a separate bowl, mix the flour and baking powder.  Gradually add the flour mixture to the butter mixture. Stir in the apricots and crumbled marzipan.

 

Preheat the oven to 350° F and cover cookie sheet with parchment paper.  Pinch off tablespoon-sized balls of the dough.  Place them on the parchment paper and flatten them with your finger.  Bake for 14 minutes, until just golden.

 

For the glaze, there are 3 options:

 

(1) Leave the cookies the way they are - golden und sunny.

(2) Melt about 1/3 cup of chocolate chips in the microwave.  Brush the tops with a little chocolate and sprinkle with a few sliced almonds.

(3) The adult version:  Mix powdered sugar with spoonfuls of Schnapps until you have a thin glaze to drizzle over the warm cookies.
